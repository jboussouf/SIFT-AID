# Deutsch's Refinements to Critical Rationalism

David Deutsch extends Popper's framework, particularly around the nature of explanation and the reach of knowledge.

## Good Explanations

The key criterion for theory selection: **hard to vary**.

A good explanation is one where all its details play a functional role - you cannot change them without destroying the explanation. Bad explanations can be easily modified to accommodate any observation (ad hoc).

**Example**: "The gods did it" explains nothing because it can explain anything. The gods' motives can always be adjusted. Contrast with explanations in physics where changing any detail produces testable consequences.

### Reach

Good explanations have *reach* - they apply beyond their original domain. Newton's laws, discovered for planetary motion, also explain tides, projectiles, and bridge construction. This reach is not designed in; it emerges from the explanation being true (or close to true).

Explanations with reach are preferable because:
1. They unify disparate phenomena
2. Their reach is evidence of truth (a false theory is unlikely to work in unintended domains)
3. They expose themselves to more opportunities for refutation

## Problems Are Soluble

All problems that are not forbidden by the laws of physics are soluble, given the right knowledge. There are no inherent limits to what can be understood or achieved - only our current ignorance.

This is not optimism but a consequence of the universality of explanation: if something can happen, it can be understood and (in principle) controlled.

## The Beginning of Infinity

Progress has no natural endpoint. Each problem solved creates new problems, new possibilities, new knowledge to be created. Stasis is not stability but decay.

This applies to:
- Scientific knowledge
- Moral knowledge
- Artistic creation
- Technological capability

## Fallibilism Strengthened

Deutsch emphasizes: we not only *might* be wrong, we almost certainly *are* wrong in ways we don't yet understand. This is not pessimism - it's the recognition that our current best theories are waypoints, not destinations.

Crucially, there is **no limit to the size of error** we can make. We cannot bound our potential wrongness - an error might be small and local, or it might be a fundamental misconception affecting everything built upon it. This is why criticism must be ongoing and no belief is beyond question.

The correct response is not paralysis but continued conjecture and criticism.
